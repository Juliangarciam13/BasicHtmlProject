Marathon

The Marathon is the athletic test par excellence, highly demanding and long distance. This foot race has
a total distance of 42 km and 195 meters and today enjoys great popularity throughout the world.
Carrying out this test with full guarantees of finishing it or getting a good mark implies good
preparation, both at the training level and at the nutritional level. The specific training sessions to
prepare for the marathon begin months before, it is also at this time that diet must be taken care of especially
in order to maintain a good state of health and to be able to perform and recover in the most optimal way.
In this article and video you will learn how the marathon diet should be.


¿What nutritional aspects should be taken into account in training?

  • Make a varied and balanced diet, that is, make a correct daily intake of energy
    and liquids, both in quality and quantity, always adapted and personalized to the
    characteristics of each athlete.

  • The needs of carbohydrates (cereals, rice, legumes, bread, pasta, potato ...) are increased,
    the diet will be high in this nutrient and the recommendation is based on ingesting throughout
    the day between 5-7 g / kg of body weight, when the workouts last one hour; and above 7 g per kilo
    if they exceed the hour of duration. The goal is to be able to perform at its best, delay the onset
    of tiredness and fatigue, recover glycogen stores after training and, therefore, reduce the physical
    stress of the body.

  • Proper hydration before, during and after training, with water or isotonic drinks If the training lasts
    more than 90 minutes, it will favor that the runner has a high performance and that he can better tolerate
    the consumption of liquid during the marathon.

  • Increased risk of iron loss, especially in women, vegetarians
    and people who eat just calories and little varied.

  • MSome people preparing for the marathon aim to adjust the percentage of body fat, in these cases, the carbohydrate
    intake is 3-5 g / kg of body weight and does not cover the recommendations described above. Thus, training may not
    be so demanding and performance will not be the priority objective. The restriction of caloric intake will be based
    above all on the reduction of fat consumption; Likewise, an intake of carbohydrates at each meal and
    an adequate consumption of proteins and micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) should be ensured.

¿How to avoid stomach problems in the marathon?

  • Make the last solid meal at least 3 hours before the marathon.

  • Test tolerance to different foods and avoid foods rich in fiber,
    fat or protein before the test.

  • Pathletes who tend to suffer serious gastrointestinal problems should
    initially try drinks with a carbohydrate concentration of 2-4% and when
    tolerance is good, progressively increase the concentration of carbohydrates
    to 4-8%.

Diet for the marathon

  • The 2-3 days prior to the marathon it is advisable to significantly reduce physical
    exercise and perform a carbohydrate overload of 9-12 g / kg of body weight to cover
    energy needs and fill glycogen stores to the maximum. Thus, the day before abundant
    carbohydrates will be taken, a certain protein load, digestible and with little fat
    (chicken or white fish) and fatty foods will be avoided as they slow digestion, spicy,
    fibrous and ferment in the intestine such as whole foods, vegetables and legumes.
    Drink water throughout the day.

  • On the day of the competition you should never try any new food, neither at breakfast
    nor in the nutritional strategies that will later be followed in the course of the marathon
    since the risk of possible gastrointestinal discomfort must be avoided. Breakfast should be
    made about 3 hours before starting the test, should contain between 100 and 300 g of carbohydrates,
    and should consist of foods with low fat and fiber content, in addition to avoiding foods that can
    cause flatulence, irritating or spicy.

    Examples of breakfast would be:

    1. Two skimmed yogurts with jam and cereals, a banana.
    2. Peach juice, white bread with honey and 0% fresh cheese, one apple.
    3. Mango juice, a skimmed yogurt with cereals and white bread with quince.

Hydration during the marathon

In the first hour of the race you can drink 300 ml of water or isotonic drink and, depending on the weight of the
athlete, between 30 and 60 g of carbohydrates every hour, the best tolerated products are gels and isotonic drink.
After 60 minutes, it is advisable to hydrate with isotonic drink that will provide carbohydrates and electrolytes
that are lost with sweat, such as sodium. Keep in mind that the increase in body temperature that occurs when running
this test, makes the risk of dehydration high, thus increasing cardiovascular stress on the body. Therefore, fluid
intake will be between 400 and 800 ml / hour, but these recommendations will always have to be adjusted individually
since sweating varies between each athlete according to; Body size, degree of training, sex, running pace and
environmental conditions.
It is very important not to overestimate sweat losses, slower, smaller runners or those who take 4 hours or more to
reach the goal, should not exceed fluid intake and your recommendation will be around 400 ml / hour. You have to drink
as you lose fluid and not ingest it above sweat losses. Therefore, it is essential that athletes adopt strategies that
allow them to become familiar with their sweat rate, such as weighing themselves before and after competition. In some runners,
the intake of small doses of caffeine consumed in cola, sports drinks or in some gels, can be beneficial in the last hour
of competition.

¿What to eat after the marathon?

Once the marathon is over, attention must be paid to the recovery to ensure that it is as complete as possible. At the
nutritional level, it is recommended to drink a shake with a carbohydrate/protein ratio of 4:1 and with sodium content,
always within an hour after the end of the competition. Then, the food should guarantee the restoration of the hydromineral
balance, be rich in vegetables and fruit to reduce acidosis, contain an abundance of carbohydrates to recover glycogen levels
and some protein food to promote protein synthesis.
